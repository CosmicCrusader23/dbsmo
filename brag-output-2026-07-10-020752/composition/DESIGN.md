# DBSMO Training — Momentum Cut

## Brand truth

Use the source app's cool light canvas, inky typography, cyan progress language, pink action state, purple selection state, and green success state. The real `public/logo.png` is the brand asset. Product UI reproductions use Inter; exterior advertising statements use Archivo Black only where the contrast creates a decisive sports-product-film register.

## Frame composition

Keep the `#f2f2f7` canvas across the whole piece, but use an oversized navy hero panel and navy product shadows to give the light app a cinematic frame. Every scene needs a visible grid/curve/telemetry layer, actual product content, and at least one foreground data accent. Do not fall back to generic glowing blobs or equalizer motifs.

## Motion language

Fluid but not bouncy. Use `power3.out` / `power4.out` for arrivals, `expo.out` only for the hero streak, `sine.inOut` for low-band atmosphere, and quick `power3.in` exits. The recognisable app-language is a 12px rise/soft scale on UI, amplified to a frame-scale camera push for the advertisement.

## Do

- Make the live math preview, ring fill, and correct feedback feel tactile and central.
- Use one strong cyan, pink, purple, or green state at a time.
- Let the final MO mark sit crisp and readable after its landing.

## Do not

- Use pure white or black as a default surface.
- Use generic stock gradients, particles, or unrelated abstract technology imagery.
- Treat the video as a static web page or a list of feature cards.
