# Brag Plan: DBSMO Training Platform

## What is this app?
DBSMO Training Platform is a self-paced Diocesan Boys' School mathematics olympiad training app with problem sets, automatic grading, progress tracking, practice mode, class assignments, writeups, feedback, and teacher analytics.

## The angle
Treat a school math practice platform like a 2016 startup that just announced a fictional Series A. The comedy is not loud; it is the straight-faced investor language applied to a very specific product: answer-only olympiad sets, KaTeX previews, streak feedback, and cohort analytics.

## Hook (first 2-3 seconds)
"DBS math olympiad training has entered its Series A era." Full-screen announcement copy lands like a funding press release.

## Key moments (the middle)
- The real sign-in/product copy: "sign in to proceed." and "Diocesan Boys' School math olympiad training."
- Practice mode choosing a category, typing `5sqrt(2)-7`, and seeing a rendered math preview.
- The correctness result: "Correct. Streak 3."
- Teacher-style metrics: attempted sets, best average, latest score, open reports, topic accuracy, and cohort snapshot.

## Outro / punchline
"DBSMO Training Platform. Fictional funding. Actual grading."

## User flow worth showing
Student signs in -> chooses a practice category -> enters an answer with live math preview -> receives correctness/streak feedback -> teacher sees cohort progress.

## Tone
- Preset: yc-parody
- Creative direction: fake Series A launch from 2016
- Interpretation: Structured, serious, metric-forward, and deliberately over-legitimate. Use startup deck language, restrained motion, hard cuts, and clean 2016 SaaS polish.
